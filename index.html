Rachel S. Y. Chen is a sonic explorer and an architect of spontaneous connection across diverse worlds. Trained in classical music but driven by improvisation, they have played in international collectives across diverse sonic genres, including experimental, pop, jazz, funk, Hip-Hop, EDM, and folk. Beyond live music, Rachel designs interactive environments where sound and movement become playgrounds for collective improvisation. Their artistic practice is deeply informed by their research on neurodiversity, where communicative diversity—moving beyond speech—is embraced as a valid and meaningful way of connecting. By exploring how sound, gesture, and other nonverbal expression can form rich dialogues, Rachel challenges conventional notions of interaction, advocating for a world where multiple modes of expression coexist freely. They believe music is not just performance but dialogue: a bridge that dissolves barriers between genres, cultures, and lived experiences. Their work invites people to step beyond the familiar, to listen deeply, and to create together, proving that even the most divergent worlds can find harmony.

“My tiny space” (2025)
What does it mean to connect with another human being? Connection begins with creating a tiny space: a gentle, intentional opening where difference can be held, and lived experiences can meet. This audio piece weaves together two musical moments born from the world of non-speaking autistic individuals and their families. The first sample features a field recording from the Magical Musical Mat (MMM), an interactive environment designed to foster connection through touch and music. In this excerpt, Arianna and Rachel—two of the MMM’s co-creators—spontaneously interact with one another by touching hands.
